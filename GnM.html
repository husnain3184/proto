Report on Business Magazine

Report on Business magazine mobile website serves its readers with the most updated information and best in long-form business stories in Canada. The team redesigned the site to improve customer engagement.

The Challenge

ROB website redesign was my graduate studies’ milestone project. In a team of three, I assumed the roles of UX designer and Project Manager.
The project’s key objective was to determine how to increase readership, site engagement, usability and an experience that is similar to its other platforms.

From the gathered business requirements we set our project goals to be:

  1. Create a new user experience for the ROB Magazine for the mobile platform.
  2. Format mobile layouts that speak to the bespoke character of the ROB.
  3. Design to increase reader engagement and loyalty, hence increasing the number of readers on the main ROB Magazine landing page.
  4. Develop features, constraints and requirements for the new concept.

Research

The team analyzed ROB magazine’s current website and of its competitors who provided similr services:

Competitor Analysis: Listing top 10 Globe and Mail competitors we, drew up a comparison chart and studied trends and ways of presenting information. From information architecture to features used for attracting readers and subcribers to analyzing the usability constraints, we detailed everything and presented to our clients.

Internal Analysis: We conducted a usability test with some of our colleagues to identify the pain points of current ROB magazine site and to learn wabout site bounce rate and time spent by readers on a particular page and story. In tandem, we carried out a SWOT (Strength, Weakness, Opportunity & Threat) analysis which made it easy for us to rethink and design for the experience.

Wireframes & Mockups

This section highlights different features that the team focuse on.

1. Landing Page:
The magazine’s landing page was redesigned considering the following aspects; page hierarchy, using direct images, proper categorization, increase traffic through landing page, clarity in graphics and subscripton tags.

2. Subscription:
Redesigning addressed issues like; a forced paywall and payment for subscription which turned away many readers who wanted to see the value of the magazine before investing into it. Also, having a lot of text and call to action buttons made it further frustrating and created an inconsistent experience within the magazine.

3. Article Pages:
With article pages, our aim was to create a unique layout that leverages the bespoke features of the print and to boost engagement by giving readers some control, use of more interactive visuals, reduce or smartly cover ads and include proper headlines and summaries which would cut down continuous text flow. 4. Article Recirculation:
Recirculation means, how often the readers click on other artciles from one article. This determines user engagement and hence time spent on with the site which, was significantly low. The only way to go to another article was to go back to categories on homepage or, search.
