The Globe & Mail

Report on Business Magazine

Report on Business magazine mobile website serves its readers with the most updated information and best in long-form business stories in Canada. ROB website redesign was my graduate studies’ milestone project. In a team of three, I assumed the roles of UX designer and Project Manager.

Reasearch

Project’s objective was to determine ways to increase readership, site engagement, usability. With the business requirements we set our project goals to be:

  1. Create a new user experience for the ROB Magazine for the mobile platform.
  2. Format mobile layouts that speak to the bespoke character of the ROB.
  3. Design to increase reader engagement and loyalty, hence increasing the number of readers on the main ROB Magazine landing page.
  4. Develop features, constraints and requirements for the new concept.

Methods:
Competitor Analysis: Listing top 10 Globe and Mail competitors, we drew up a comparison chart and studied trends and ways of presenting information. From information architecture to features used for attracting readers and subcribers to analyzing the usability constraints, we detailed everything and presented to our clients.

Internal Analysis: We conducted a usability test with some of our colleagues to identify the pain points of current ROB magazine site and to learn wabout site bounce rate and time spent by readers on a particular page and story. In tandem, we carried out a SWOT (Strength, Weakness, Opportunity & Threat) analysis which made it easy for us to rethink and design for the experience.

Structuring

Storyboarding

This phase began with the team sorting out all the features that could be improved or improvised in the mobile site to achieve our objectives. We started off by sorting and prioritizing the low hanging fruits and others that needed some detailed work.

Wireframes & Mockups

This section highlights different features that the team focused on.

1. Landing page:

Original design
  • Text heavy
  • Hard to follow
  • No content hierarchy
  • No filters
  • No categorization
Redesign (2 iterations)
  • Use of images with text
  • Group content
  • Different font weights
  • Highlighted story
  • Better content organization

2. Subscription:

Original design
  • Forced paywall and payment for subcription turned away many readers.
  • Various call to actions buttons
  • No visual cues
  • Text heavy page
Redesign (2 iterations)
  • Gave a brief of story before showing the paywall. This gave readers a sense of value of their subscription
  • Clear and organized CTAs
  • Use of color and font to grab reader’s attention
  • Visually distinguished content
  • 2nd iteration had more use of color and font weight for visual distinction

3. Article Pages:

Redesign
  • Designs aimed at creating a layout that leveraged bespoke features of print
  • Added static images and interactive visual components which gave readers the control over content and made the article more engaging
  • Reduced or covered ads
  • Included headlines and summaries to cut down continuous flow of text and improve readability

4. Article Recirculation:

Original design

Recirculation means, how often the readers click and move to other articles from one article. This determines user engagement and time spent with the site. This number was significantly low as the original layout did not show any other related articles or connection between pages. The only way to jump to a different article was to go back to categories on homepage or search.

Redesign

The idea was to add a collapsable section that would show more and related articles to the reader. I created two variations of the screen, the first one shows one or two articles at a time, depending on the orientation, and the other displayed multiple articles from different categories.
