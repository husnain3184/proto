Personalized Engagement Metrics

A web application that measures the levels of engagement and trust between a financial advisor and their clients. This proof of concept was designed for a Toronto based company called Veriday.

The Initial Dive

The devil always lies in the details, hence, getting our initial research and analysis as detailed as possible was the first step in our process.



Competitve Analysis - The first essential step was to have a clear vision of our end users’ industry; their requirements, practices and pain points before embarking on the product development cycle.
The team conducted a competitive analysis of known financial institutions. This gave us a high-level overview of where the industry stands today, who the regulators and trendsetters are, and details about the workforce.


Interviews and Surveys - This technique have us detailed insights and understanding of the relation between our two major stakeholders; financial advisors and the clients.
Various professional financial advisors were interviewed and surveyed, along with people who consulted or were looking to consult an advisor.
The team designed two separate questionnaires for participants: one to better understand the industry and how financial advisors build relationships with their clientele; and the second to collect data about client experiences with their financial advisors. In total, four advisors and one client was digitally interviewed.

Snapshot from the compiled research

User Personas

The data from our research was used to create three our user personas, with different individual characteristics and values for example; personality type, goals, experience and use of technology.

Structuring

Before starting any design, we spent considerable time in building sense of the workflows and end user journeys. Main use cases in consideration were ‘ An advisor logging-in through vTrust’ and ‘An advisor logging-in using a digital agent (assuming the compatibility)’. We drew an experience map to judge our concept in terms of emotional design, human behaviour, motivation & engagement and mental models.
